Abstract

Despite recent advances in image editing technologies, such as generative adversarial networks (GANs), the challenge of enabling flexible and iterative modifications of image content remains, particularly within the fashion industry. Fashion image editing requires nuanced modifications in color and texture, a feat which current methods often fall short of achieving. In this paper, we introduce CoDE-GAN, a novel method that allows for flexible image content editing guided by sketches, while ensuring the generation of result images with consistent colors and textures. The core of our proposed solution lies in the decoupling of content and texture representation, a strategy aimed at overcoming the reconstruction challenges posed by sketch-contoured content regions. This is achieved through the integration of a content decoupling module into CoDE-GAN. This module pre-processes inputs with contour-guidance and appearance-guidance, transforming the guidance into separate content and texture representations. For further enhancement of color consistency and texture refinement, we employ a content enhancement module. This module, applied to the decoder, computes a content response map and uses cosine similarity to constrain it to a gray image. As a result, CoDE-GAN accomplishes coarse-to-fine results in a single stage, enhancing both the editing flexibility and the quality of the generated images. Through extensive experiments conducted on three diverse datasets, including the fashion human ATR dataset, the garment-based dataset, and a scene dataset, we demonstrate that CoDE-GAN outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods. This paper, therefore, represents a significant step forward in the quest for advanced, nuanced, and flexible image editing methodologies.
